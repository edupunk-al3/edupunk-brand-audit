## Origin Story

Over a month of integrating AI deeply into my creative workflow, three convictions crystallized:

- **Context assets must be stored locally.** Never locked in a single model's cloud. Must be portable across platforms.
- **If AI is your scaffolding, it must deliver 10x better outcomes.** And translate into real-world action scenarios.
- **Structures created with AI should be open-sourced immediately.** This is not individual intelligence. Don't hoard it.

Brand asset mapping and auditing became the first scenario where I found a definitive, 10x improvement in both efficiency and outcomes.

## What You'll Get

Through a 3-30 hour brand audit journey, you'll excavate the possibilities of your own style, explore your brand's contours, and receive two documents:

- **Document 1: Raw Interview Data** — rich with clues, open and warm, preserving the full dialogue flow. For future reference, iteration, and management.
- **Document 2: Brand Context Profile** — a brand specification framework designed for LLM generation and design. For quickly loading brand context into different conversations.

## Design Rationale

### Calibration: Your Distance from "Brand"

Before anything begins, the framework calibrates the distance between you and what you mean by "brand."

Everyone's capacity and development stage is different. You need to recognize whether you are:

- **Trying to turn yourself into a brand** (Mode A — Personal Brand)
- **Managing a brand** (Mode B — Brand Steward)
- **Hoping a brand will gradually grow from who you are** (Mode C — Founder in Transition)

The action strategy for each relationship is fundamentally different. The framework determines your mode through two calibration questions and dynamically adjusts throughout the interview.

---

### Journey 1: North Star & Identity Positioning

Approximately 18 questions, focused on:

- Which brands and creators you respect and admire
- Who inspired those brands and creators
- Whether there are converging threads and sources
- What industry game you're currently in
- How to create positive externalities for your industry
- Who you are, whose interests you represent, what you want to be known for
- Your Ikigai intersection

At the close of Journey 1, the framework forms an initial brand archetype hypothesis based on your inner flame and desire structure (**Anchor Point 1**).

#### The Brand Twelve Archetypes

The archetype system used here is rooted in Jungian psychology and developed for brand application by Margaret Mark & Carol S. Pearson. Two axes, four quadrants, twelve archetypes:

**Two Axes:**
- Horizontal: Belonging/Connection <-> Independence/Self-Actualization
- Vertical: Stability/Control <-> Risk/Mastery

**Key principles for use:**

1. Feeling drawn to multiple archetypes is healthy — it indicates balanced desire structure
2. The archetype grid is a reference frame, not a compass — the compass is always the person's inner flame
3. The same brand may have different archetypes at different development stages
4. Different people may assign different archetypes to the same brand — this reveals the judge's desire structure
5. At any given time, a brand has one primary archetype (one voice); consumers activate different archetypes in different contexts
6. Archetypes are the starting point, not the destination — they must be integrated with vision, mission, and values

### Journey 2: Meaning Engineering

Approximately 55 questions spanning four dimensions:

**Beliefs & Contrarian Takes**
- What you believe that others don't
- Industry cliches in your field
- Your philosophical positions

**Expression Mechanics**
- How you actually communicate (not how you think you do)
- What forms of expression you're naturally skilled at
- Your word preferences

**Aesthetic Crimes**
- Brand behaviors that make you physically uncomfortable
- Content that feels lazy or uninspired

**Voice & Personality**
- How your humor manifests
- How you handle controversial topics
- Deeper personality excavation

After Journey 2, the second archetype calibration occurs (**Anchor Point 2**). Each judgment invites your feedback — this creates a dialogue interface, not a psychological assessment. Treat each archetype judgment as a thread for thinking, not a label to accept.

---

### Journey 3: Brand Practice

Approximately 35 questions focused on brand communication practice:

**Content Rhythm**
- How you organize content across different time scales
- How you handle the relationship between long-form and short-form

**Hard Nos**
- Expressions you absolutely won't make
- Activities you absolutely won't do
- People or brands you absolutely won't associate with

**Red Flags**
- What makes you immediately distrust a brand
- What "fake" looks like in your industry
- The difference between growth and imitation

The final archetype confirmation occurs (**Anchor Point 3**), and two complete documents are generated.

## Output Documents

### Document 1: Raw Interview Data

A verbatim transcript of the entire interview — every question, every answer, word for word. Including calibration questions, follow-ups, your hesitations and corrections. No editing, summarizing, or reorganizing.

Serves as the "raw material library" for your brand context. Can be used for regeneration, secondary analysis, or fed to different LLMs for different purposes.

### Document 2: Brand Context Profile

A refined and structured reference document derived from Document 1. It's designed for LLMs, not humans — serving as the "context base" for all brand-related conversations. Whenever you need an LLM to write brand content, analyze decisions, or discuss brand topics, provide this document as context.
